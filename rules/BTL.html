BTL:

  • §1
    Drivers have to follow international traffic laws and use common sense on public roads. The BTL, which includes the VCG, is only active on public roads and areas.
    • §1.1
      Common sense is a major factor on our roads. There are many unwritten rules.
  • §2
    All rules in the BTL have to be followed by all traffic members. Police officers and civilians can report traffic violations.
  • §3
    There are rules which can overwrite other rules. Any rule can be overwriten, if another rule says something different. Please note that judges and police officers might understand the rules differently. Just use your common sense and justice will be on your side. Everyone driver has to be able to understand the rules. Feel free to ask an police officer or moderator if you aren't able to understand a rule.
  • §4
    Traffic signs can be ignored if they don't make sense. Otherwise Players have to follow the instructions given by those.

Intersections and right of way:

  • §101
    We are using a modified version of the traffic laws from the USA here. Cars have to stop, yield and/or continue into the intersection according to the type of intersection they are facing.
  • §102
    Intersection with no traffic lights, signs or similar can be adressed as "unregulated intersections". Don't be confused by that name, these intersections are regulated, but in a light manner.
    • §102.1
      Unregulated Intersections have to be slowly approached, vehicles don't have to come to an complete halt. The drivers will then continue, in the order in which they arrived. The first driver to approach the intersection will have to right to use the intersection first. Drivers don't have to yield for traffic, approaching the intersection from the front.
  • §103
    Traffic lights are not working properly, therefore we are ignoring them. But they act like stop-signs for us.
    • §103.1
      This means that all traffic has to come to an complete halt before continuing.
  • §104
    Edge lines indicate that an road has the right of way. Traffic from side roads has to yield for the vehicles which are using the lane with the edge line. Drivers don't have to come to an complete stop here.
  • §105
    U-Turns are generaly not allowed.
  • §106
    You have to yield for vehicles coming from the front, if you want to turn left.
  • §107
    It is allowed to cross solid and double solid lines, if you are turning. Some intersections feature these lines, so you can ignore them when turning.
  • §108
    Drivers who want to park or maneuvor their vehicle/combination around have to yield for all other traffic and not block their path.
    • §108.1
      Drivers who already began their maneuvor or parking process, after following the above, have the right of way and can continue their move even when other traffic is approaching. All other traffic has to yield and keep safe distance from the maneuvering vehicle/combination.

Parking:

  • §201
    It's not allowed to park on main or priority roads.
  • §202
    It's not allowed to park on highways, or 10 meters next to a highway. The same goes for freeways.
  • §203
    Parking on roads is only allowed if you're not slowing down the traffic flow.

Speed:

  • §301
    The speed limit intercity is 60 km/h (about 40 mph). Only excessive speeding is a crime.
  • §302
    Vehicles are allowed to cruise with a maximum of 100 km/h outside of cities.
  • §303
    There is no speed limit for high-, free- and speedways.
  • §304
    Drivers are never allowed to drive faster than their vehicle or combination can handle.

Heavy Combinations:

  • §601
    Vehicles and/or combinations with a combined weight of 8 tons or more fall under the rules below. The DOTAS decided to make special laws for these.
  • §602
    Heavy Combinations have to stay on the rightmost lane when using rural roads, high- and speedways.
  • §603
    Trucks are dangerous at high speed. Therefore drivers are not allowed to drive faster than the DOTAS suggestes.
    • §604.1
      Vehicles and combinations with a weight between 8 and 25 tons are only allowed to drive with a speed of up to 80 km/h (about 50 mph).
    • §604.2
      Vehicles and combinations with a weight of over 25 tons are only allowed to drive with a speed of up to 60 km/h.
